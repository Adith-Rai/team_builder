THE GOAL — re-read every time you make a decision
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

We are building a Pokemon battle AI that competes at an elite level — among
the strongest human players, the strongest published bots (Metamon's Kakuna,
SmallRL, Minikazam), and the strongest other engines.

**Ultimate scope (load-bearing):** elite play across **every legal Pokemon
format** — singles, doubles, triples, 4v4, 6v6, all tiers (OU, Ubers, AG,
UU, RU, etc.), random battles (which have varied levels), in-game/cartridge
battles. All gens (4-9+, expanding as new gens release). Plus team
building, not just play.

**Deployment scope:** must scale **massively on paid cloud** for throughput-
heavy training AND remain **runnable locally** on a 6 GB consumer GPU for
development + smoke tests. Same code, both environments. Not parallel
codebases. See `memory/project_vision_scope.md` for full constraints.

V1 baseline is singles gen 9 — but every refactor decision must NOT
foreclose paths to the broader scope. If a shortcut hardcodes gen9ou or
6v6, surface the tradeoff explicitly before shipping.

Elite Pokemon play is about long-horizon planning, opponent modeling,
risk-adjusted decision making, and squeezing every fractional advantage
from type/STAB/effectiveness/speed/items/abilities/positioning.

CARRY-OVER PRACTICES (lessons paid for in prior sessions)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. **Read docs + code BEFORE estimating scope.** Sessions 49 + 50 both burned
   evenings because of "should be a quick fix" estimates that ignored real
   complexity. Trust line-cited audits, not first-impression reads.

2. **Verify before recommending from memory.** A memory record naming a
   function/flag is a claim about a past moment, not current state. Grep
   for it before promising it works.

3. **Smoke ALWAYS before launching long runs.** Session 50 caught multiple
   bugs that would have wasted 40+ hours each if launched blind:
   - V9RLPlayer kwarg mismatch
   - return-tuple shape
   - opp_records format
   - mp+pipeline GPU contention deadlock

4. **`--mp` is transformer-only.** Legacy ckpts (sp_NNNN, BattleAgent class)
   work as PFSP opponents (factory dispatches via `is_transformer_checkpoint`)
   but cannot be `--init-from` for `--mp`.

5. **Cloud: Network Volume MANDATORY for any run > 1 epoch.** Container Disk
   doesn't survive `podStop+podResume` if pod re-provisions on a different
   host. Lost a partial BC run this way (Session 49).

6. **Don't rush refactors under time pressure.** "Just ship it tonight" is
   the pattern that produces next session's surprise gaps. Stop at clean
   breakpoints if needed.

7. **Backward compat is a hard constraint, not a nice-to-have.** Legacy PPO
   ckpts must remain loadable as PFSP opponents in every future run. The
   `is_transformer_checkpoint` factory-dispatch invariant protects this.
